Claims with dates 
#################

Dates, specifically time-bound claims, are the central focus of the Security Force Monitor's data and methodology. All claims in the Security Force Monitor dataset are time-bound. Sometimes the dates for claims are clearly stated in citations and the methodology for structuring these claims into time-bound data is simple. Often, however, citations contain ambigious claims about dates which need careful consideration to properly capture as data.

All dates should be entered in the YYYY-MM-DD format. We structure all dates into ranges with a first and last date. This means even a single day, such as 5 May 2025 would be entered with a first date of 2025-05-05 and a last date of 2025-05-05. Ranges can be precise, meaning details of the claim were true for all dates in the range. Or ranges can be imprecise, meaning details of the claim were true for at least one date in the range.

In a single claim any precise ranges should be contained within the imprecise range.

From June 5th 2012 to April 19th 2015 he was commander of the 1st Battalion. Afterwards he served in variety of positions: head of the Officer Training School, deputy commander of the 4th Brigade, and section coordinator for Operation Enforcement.
